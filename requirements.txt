# 1. Education Match (30% weight)
# This measures how closely the applicant’s educational background matches the job requirement.
# What it checks:
# Degree level (e.g., Bachelor’s, Master’s)
# Field of study (e.g., IT, Computer Science, Engineering)
# Required vs. preferred education

# Why it’s 30%:
# Education sets the foundation for the role. If the job explicitly requires a certain degree or field, 
# this becomes a strong indicator of baseline suitability.

# Example:
# Required: BS in IT
# Applicant: BS in Computer Science → High score
# Applicant: BA in History → Low score

# 2. Years of Experience (25% weight)
# This evaluates how many years of professional experience the applicant has compared to what the job requires.

# What it checks:

# Total years in relevant roles
# Minimum experience threshold

# Why it’s 25%:
# Experience often correlates with practical exposure and maturity, but it’s slightly less important than education fit in roles that require formal training.

# Example:
# Required: 3 years
# Applicant has 5 years → Full or near-full score
# Applicant has 1 year → Partial score

# 3. Work Experience Relevance (25% weight)
# This measures how closely the applicant’s past job roles align with the current job description.
# What it checks:
# Similar job titles
# Similar responsibilities

# Industry relevance
# Overlap in tools, systems, or technologies

# Why it’s 25%:
# Not all experience is equal. Someone with fewer years but highly relevant experience can outperform someone with many years in unrelated roles.

# Example:
# Job: Laravel Developer
# Applicant worked as a PHP/Laravel Developer → High score
# Applicant worked as a Network Technician → Low score

# 4. General Qualifications (20% weight)
# This covers additional requirements and soft/technical skills not captured above.
# What it includes:

# Certifications
# Technical skills (e.g., SQL, Laravel, Python)
# Soft skills (communication, leadership)
# Training, seminars, licenses

# Why it’s 20%:
# These factors enhance a candidate’s profile but usually don’t override core requirements like education or experience.

# Example:
# Has required certifications → Higher score

# Lacks key skills → Lower score
